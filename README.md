# Zomato Business Analysis using PostgreSQL

## Project Overview

This project is a comprehensive business analysis of a **Zomato-inspired food delivery platform** using **PostgreSQL**. It focuses on solving real-world business problems by querying relational datasets and extracting actionable insights through advanced SQL techniques.

The project simulates the responsibilities of a **Business Analyst/Data Analyst** by analyzing customer behavior, restaurant performance, rider efficiency, revenue trends, order patterns, and operational metrics directly from transactional data.

The analysis is performed entirely in **PostgreSQL** using advanced SQL queries without relying on visualization tools, demonstrating strong database querying and analytical problem-solving skills.

---

## Objectives

* Perform end-to-end business analysis using PostgreSQL.
* Analyze customer purchasing behavior and lifetime value.
* Evaluate restaurant performance and revenue generation.
* Measure rider efficiency and delivery performance.
* Identify customer churn and segmentation opportunities.
* Analyze seasonal demand and ordering trends.
* Generate business insights using advanced SQL techniques.

## Business Problems Solved

This project answers **20 real-world business questions**, including:

1. Top 5 most frequently ordered dishes by a customer.
2. Identification of peak ordering time slots.
3. Average order value for high-frequency customers.
4. High-value customers based on total spending.
5. Orders placed but not delivered.
6. Restaurant revenue ranking within each city.
7. Most popular dish in every city.
8. Customer churn analysis.
9. Restaurant cancellation rate comparison across years.
10. Rider average delivery time analysis.
11. Monthly restaurant growth ratio.
12. Customer segmentation into Gold and Silver categories.
13. Monthly rider earnings estimation.
14. Rider performance rating based on delivery time.
15. Peak ordering day for every restaurant.
16. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).
17. Monthly sales trend analysis.
18. Rider efficiency comparison.
19. Seasonal popularity of menu items.
20. City-wise revenue ranking.
